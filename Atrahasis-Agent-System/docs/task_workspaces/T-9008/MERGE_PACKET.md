# Anu Round-Four Merge Packet

## Decision

No new doctrine inventions.
I think we should do it like this:

- Keep the doctrine locked as `closed capability, open accountability`.
- Keep the four-surface architecture: `Sanctum`, `Foundry`, `Enterprise`,
  `Public`.
- Do not sell raw AGI access.
- Keep recursion-critical novelty under internal embargo by default.
- Use staged model sovereignty.
- Rewrite `C14`, `C15`, `C18`, and `C22` first.
- Accept `C48 Glass Vault` only as a bounded cryptographic accountability
  layer.
- Do not describe `C48` as proving that the AGI is globally safe.
- Describe `C48` as proving narrow, machine-checkable compliance facts:
  release-law execution, refusal-path activation, audit-log commitments, policy
  version anchors, and lineage attestations.

## Why My Choice Refines But Does Not Change

- `AAT 3` confirms strategic convergence.
- `C48` improves the accountability side of the doctrine if scoped correctly.
- `C48` becomes dangerous if it overclaims blanket proof of safety or alignment.
- So the right move is not to reject `C48`, but to narrow it and integrate it
  with existing provenance and authority layers.
