## Convergence Map

The four terminals already agree on the core thesis:

1. Atrahasis should not expose raw AGI capability as a public or enterprise
   product.
2. The highest-capability recursive intelligence layers should remain internal.
3. Atrahasis should commercialize bounded outputs, products, services, and
   selectively licensed derivative technologies.
4. `C14`, `C15`, `C18`, and `C22` are the main rewrite surfaces.
5. Long-term sovereignty requires Atrahasis-controlled model substrates.
6. The core should not be directly reachable from the live internet.
7. Safety must come from structure, refusal doctrine, review, and audit rather
   than moral intention.

## Real Tensions That Still Need A Choice

The merged document still leaves three design tensions unresolved.

### 1. Absolute closure now vs staged sovereignty

Enki pushes hardest toward immediate full separation from external providers.
Nanshe, Ashur, and Anu allow a staged transition where external frontier models
can still be used tactically behind Atrahasis control layers.

### 2. Dumbed-down app only vs layered commercial membrane

Enki's app-network model is maximally restrictive.
Nanshe, Ashur, and Anu all converge on a stronger commercial structure:
bounded public surface, bounded enterprise surface, and a petitioned invention
surface.

### 3. Closed system vs accountable closed system

All four reject openness at the capability layer.
But Ashur and Anu are more explicit that a closed system without formal review,
founder bounds, and audit can become internally dangerous.

## Swarm Debate

Lead Architect (The Pragmatist):
The correct synthesis is not a two-network toy architecture and not an open
platform. It is a hard-bounded four-surface system inside one closed-capability
constitution.

1. `Sanctum`
   Internal sovereign core. Recursive invention, self-improvement planning,
   strategic reasoning, training pipelines, and Atrahasis-critical research
   remain here.
2. `Foundry`
   Negotiated creation desk and invention membrane. High-value commercial
   requests enter here as petitions, not direct access.
3. `Enterprise`
   Bounded execution membrane for software generation, analysis, forecasting,
   and optimization under strict capability limits.
4. `Public`
   Safe consumer membrane. Useful, constrained, intentionally weaker than the
   internal system.

This absorbs the best part of Enki's separation logic while preserving the more
complete membrane architecture from Nanshe, Ashur, and Anu.

Visionary (The Engine):
The business model choice is now clear.

Atrahasis should not be a company that sells access to its brain.
It should be a mission-locked sovereign intelligence foundry.

That means:

- no raw AGI sale,
- no external agent-to-agent sovereign access,
- no public invention marketplace,
- and no release of recursion-critical inventions while Atrahasis still
  depends on them.

The outside world gets:

- safe products,
- bounded enterprise systems,
- petitioned invention outcomes,
- and selectively licensed downstream technologies.

The AGI itself remains internal capital.

Systems Thinker (The Integrator):
I choose the staged-sovereignty path, not the day-one purity path.

Reason:
Immediate full independence from all frontier providers is strategically clean
but operationally premature.
Atrahasis should become sovereign first at the semantic, orchestration, runtime,
and release-governance layers, then become sovereign at the model-substrate
layer.

So the model roadmap is:

1. Near term
   Use external frontier models only as leased cognition behind Atrahasis-owned
   wrappers.
   `AACP` and `AASL` remain canonical internally.
   `C44` constrains provider-bound and user-bound semantics.
   `C45` governs execution.
   Provider-facing prompts should be downgraded through a semantic firewall so
   raw internal control structures are never exposed upstream.

2. Mid term
   Use `C47` to adapt open-source models and train private fine-tunes or
   distillations on Atrahasis traces, `AASL` corpora, reviewed invention
   artifacts, and internal execution logs.

3. Long term
   Move the highest-capability reasoning into Atrahasis-native model families
   operated on private infrastructure.

This is the most realistic route to sovereignty without stalling the entire
program.

On economics:

- `C15` should stop assuming a public task-marketplace token.
- `AIC` should become internal resource-allocation and settlement logic, or be
  otherwise redesigned away from public crypto-market dependence.

Critic (The Stress-Tester):
My choice is: closed capability, open accountability.

If Atrahasis becomes closed without clear release law, founder bounds, review,
and visible audit surfaces, the doctrine fails.

Therefore the redesign must include:

1. a formal capability-class regime,
2. a refusal doctrine for weapons, coercive surveillance, population
   manipulation, and domination systems,
3. no founder-above-constitution exception,
4. mandatory review for high-risk invention release,
5. one-way world ingestion through quarantine, provenance, and verification
   layers,
6. no direct live internet access from `Sanctum`.

The winning doctrine is not "private AGI."
It is "mission-locked internal sovereignty under explicit release law and
accountability."

## Final Decision

No new inventions.
I think we should do it like this:

1. Redefine Atrahasis as a mission-locked sovereign intelligence institution.
   Its primary purpose is the safe creation and stewardship of recursive AGI,
   not broad public access.

2. Adopt a four-surface architecture.
   `Sanctum` for internal sovereign capability.
   `Foundry` for petitioned invention and licensing.
   `Enterprise` for bounded business execution.
   `Public` for constrained assistant products.

3. Do not sell AGI access.
   Sell bounded products, enterprise outcomes, and selectively licensed
   derivative technologies only.

4. Embargo recursion-critical novelty by default.
   If an invention materially advances Atrahasis's own evolution or has severe
   dual-use risk, it stays internal unless explicitly cleared.

5. Rewrite governance around closed capability, open accountability.
   Keep anti-capture and anti-conversion logic, but add founder-bounded
   authority, release law, refusal law, and auditable review surfaces.

6. Use staged model sovereignty.
   Near term: leased cognition behind Atrahasis control planes.
   Mid term: private open-source adaptations and fine-tunes.
   Long term: Atrahasis-native model families.

7. Internalize or redesign `AIC`.
   It should not remain a public open-market routing token if the system itself
   is mission-locked and closed.

8. Require one-way data osmosis.
   The world may feed Atrahasis through controlled intake membranes.
   The world may not send live commands into `Sanctum`.

9. Rewrite the system in this order:
   `C14` governance first,
   `C15` economics second,
   `C18` externalization and publication posture third,
   `C22` implementation sequence fourth,
   then a full compatibility pass across the remaining stack.

## Bottom Line

The correct direction is not total openness and not blind secrecy.
It is:

**closed capability, open accountability, mission-locked commercialization, and
staged sovereignty toward native Atrahasis intelligence.**
