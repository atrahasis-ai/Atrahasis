## Second-Round Swarm Debate

## Lead Architect

The final architecture should standardize on four surfaces:

1. `Sanctum`
   Internal sovereign core.
   Recursive invention, self-improvement planning, strategic research, internal model training, and all Atrahasis-critical cognition.

2. `Foundry`
   Operator-mediated invention and licensing surface.
   External parties may petition for outcomes, but cannot drive Sanctum directly.

3. `Enterprise`
   Bounded business execution.
   Software generation, forecasting, optimization, and operational support without unrestricted novelty access.

4. `Public`
   Safe assistant membrane.
   Useful, intentionally weaker, and never a public agent-factory.

This naming scheme absorbs the best parts of all four terminal outputs and removes ambiguity.

## Visionary

Do not sell access to intelligence.
Sell constitutionally reviewed externalizations of intelligence.

The strongest converged business model is:
- public utility for broad revenue and data on safe interaction,
- enterprise bounded execution for practical recurring revenue,
- Foundry petition workflows for high-value invention,
- strategic licensing of non-recursion-critical derivatives,
- permanent internal retention of anything that materially improves Atrahasis itself.

This means Atrahasis becomes a sealed intelligence custodian with a downstream invention economy.

## Critic

The one choice I reject is premature absolutism.

A fully air-gapped final Sanctum is a valid end state.
But demanding full hard enclosure on day one while Atrahasis still depends on frontier cognition would create strategic paralysis or force fake sovereignty claims.

So the correct choice is:
- closed end state,
- staged bootstrap,
- hard prohibition on direct external control of Sanctum,
- and eventual removal of leased cognition once native sovereignty is ready.

Also:
- no founder exception,
- no opaque release decisions,
- no weapons, coercive surveillance, population manipulation, or domination contracts,
- no direct live internet browsing from Sanctum,
- no release of recursion-critical inventions without explicit constitutional review.
