# Science Assessment - C31 Crystallographic Adaptive Topology

**Assessor:** Science Advisor
**Invention:** C31 - Crystallographic Adaptive Topology
**Date:** 2026-03-11

---

## Executive Summary

C31 is scientifically and technically **SOUND at the architectural level**. It does not depend on speculative new mathematics. Its main ingredients are:

- deterministic partitioning of finite sets;
- small-group coordination principles;
- externally derived capability scoring;
- bounded optional incentives for group stability.

The proposal is strongest when treated as an additive organizational layer rather than a universal law of system topology.

**Overall soundness score: 4.0 / 5**

## Per-Claim Assessment

### Claim 1: Small deterministic neighborhoods improve local coordination recovery
**Assessment:** PARTIALLY_SOUND

Rationale:
- Small groups are easier to relearn than fully flat peer sets.
- The claim is plausible and architecturally coherent.
- The exact quantitative improvement should be treated as a validation target, not an established fact.

### Claim 2: Capability-based local roles are preferable to undifferentiated parcel peers
**Assessment:** SOUND

Rationale:
- Role specialization is broadly consistent with both organizational and systems-engineering practice.
- External derivation from historical behavior reduces gaming risk relative to self-report.

### Claim 3: Deterministic topology can coexist with elastic parceling
**Assessment:** SOUND

Rationale:
- DANs are computed from parcel state; they do not compete with parcel formation.
- This is a compositional refinement, not a contradictory structure.

### Claim 4: Economic coherence incentives can stabilize useful local structure
**Assessment:** PARTIALLY_SOUND

Rationale:
- Incentives can improve stability.
- They also risk concentration and metric-gaming, so default-off plus circuit breakers is the correct posture.

## Main Risks

1. **Measurement risk**
   - Performance benefit may be smaller than expected.

2. **Incentive risk**
   - The coherence bonus can distort behavior if enabled prematurely.

3. **Abstraction risk**
   - Operators may mistake CAT for a foundational necessity rather than an optional optimization layer.

## Scientific Verdict

**PARTIALLY_SOUND to SOUND overall, sufficient for advancement.**

There is no scientific blocker. The work required next is empirical validation of benefit and governance discipline around optional incentive mechanisms.
