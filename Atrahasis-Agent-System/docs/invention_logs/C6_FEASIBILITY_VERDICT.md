## Council Deliberation

### Advocate Position

The Epistemic Metabolism Architecture addresses a genuine, validated market gap. No existing system treats knowledge as a dynamic metabolic process with autonomous consolidation, ecological self-regulation, and multi-ontology projection. The landscape analysis confirms: the market has every individual component (persistent memory, knowledge graphs, consolidation research, context engineering) but no system synthesizes them. EMA is the synthesis.

The refined concept has honestly addressed the Science Advisor's 3.0/5 soundness rating. The epistemic quantum now has a concrete JSON schema that differentiates it from RDF triples and knowledge graph nodes. The projection claim has been weakened from "lossless" to "bounded loss" with specific fidelity guarantees per subsystem. The dreaming process is now gated through PCVM verification with a dedicated C-class claim type. SHREC stability has a Lotka-Volterra formulation with 5 independent resources providing niche differentiation and floor budget guarantees preventing competitive exclusion.

The concrete example (Section 6) demonstrates the full metabolic pipeline is not merely metaphorical — it produces a specific, traceable flow from prediction ingestion through consolidation to eventual catabolism with knowledge preservation at each stage.

The 5/5 novelty rating from ideation is warranted. No prior system has:
1. Self-projecting knowledge units with Subjective Logic confidence
2. Ecological budget regulation (SHREC) for knowledge lifecycle
3. LLM-based dreaming consolidation with verification gating
4. The unified metabolic architecture combining all three

The 12-18 month market window is real. MemOS is the closest competitor and is moving fast. EMA must advance to capture first-mover advantage in the metabolic knowledge paradigm.

**Advocate score:** Novelty 5, Feasibility 3, Impact 4.

### Skeptic Position

The Science Advisor gave this 3.0/5 for soundness, and while the mitigations improve it, they do not resolve the fundamental tension: **the metabolic metaphor is generative but not falsifiable as a theory.**

The Science Advisor asked: "Does the metabolic framing make quantitative predictions that distinguish it from alternative architectures?" The refined concept provides metabolic health metrics (Section 7) but does not answer whether those metrics would be DIFFERENT under a non-metabolic architecture that simply implements TTL-based expiry, periodic re-indexing, and query-based retrieval. If a simpler system produces the same metric profiles, the metabolic complexity is unjustified.

**Specific concerns:**

1. **Dreaming remains the highest-risk component.** The PCVM gate mitigates hallucination but the residual risk rating of 5/10 is the highest in the system. The 3-pass majority voting reduces noise but does not address systematic LLM bias. If all 3 passes hallucinate the same culturally common analogy, majority voting CONFIRMS the hallucination. The adversarial report's A3 (Consolidation Poisoning, CRITICAL) shows the dreaming process is fundamentally vulnerable to strategic manipulation.

2. **Scale is inadequately addressed.** The adversarial report's A8 (Coherence Collapse at Scale, CRITICAL) reveals that the architecture has no coherence graph sharding specification. At the target scale of C3 (1K-10K agents, with aspirational 100K), the coherence graph will contain tens to hundreds of millions of edges. The refined concept does not address this. "Define it in DESIGN" is not a mitigation — it is a deferral.

3. **The 5-signal SHREC is unproven.** The original 4-signal SHREC was already PARTIALLY_SOUND per the Science Advisor. The refined concept adds a 5th signal (Consolidation) without additional stability analysis. The Lotka-Volterra formulation is provided but the linearized stability analysis is for the general case — the SPECIFIC parameter values for the 5-signal EMA system have not been analyzed. The Lyapunov function requires alpha_ij < K_i/K_j for all i,j — this is an ASSUMPTION, not a result.

4. **Bounded-loss projections are targets, not guarantees.** The fidelity bounds (0.85-0.92) are DESIGN TARGETS. Science Advisor Experiment 2 is needed to validate them. If actual fidelity is below these targets (e.g., 0.70 for C3 round-trip), the "bounded loss" claim becomes "significant loss" and the canonical-source principle becomes a bottleneck (agents must always check canonical quanta because projections are unreliable).

5. **The metabolic advantage over simpler approaches is unproven.** Science Advisor Experiment 1 (metabolic vs. non-metabolic baseline) has not been conducted. The entire architecture may be over-engineered compared to a standard knowledge graph with TTL, periodic consolidation, and query-based retrieval. The metabolic framing adds vocabulary and complexity — it must PROVE it adds value.

**Skeptic score:** Novelty 4, Feasibility 2, Impact 3.

---

### Arbiter Resolution

The Advocate and Skeptic have legitimate positions. The Advocate correctly identifies the market gap and the architectural novelty. The Skeptic correctly identifies that novelty without proven advantage is complexity, not innovation.

**Resolution on key disputes:**

1. **Metabolic framing as theory vs. metaphor:** The Skeptic is right that the metabolic framing has not been proven to produce measurably different outcomes from simpler approaches. However, the Advocate is right that the framing GENERATES architectural features (dreaming, SHREC, vitality-based catabolism) that would not arise from conventional knowledge management design. The resolution: **the metaphor earns its keep if it produces at least one architectural feature that is not achievable by simpler means.** Dreaming consolidation with PCVM verification is that feature. No simpler knowledge management approach would produce autonomous cross-domain synthesis with verification gating.

2. **Feasibility:** The Skeptic's score of 2 is too harsh. The refined concept provides concrete schemas, formulations, and coordination mechanisms. The C5 PCVM (feasibility 3) has comparable theoretical gaps. However, the Advocate's 3 is at the upper bound — EMA has more unproven components than C5 and more critical scale questions. **Feasibility: 3.**

3. **Impact:** The Advocate's 4 is warranted IF the dreaming process produces genuine value. The Skeptic's 3 reflects the risk that dreaming may produce more noise than signal. **Impact: 4** — the metabolic paradigm, if it works, is a genuine category-creating innovation in knowledge management. The impact ceiling justifies the risk.

4. **Novelty:** Both council members agree: 4-5 range. Three components at 5/5 novelty per prior art research. **Novelty: 4** — reducing by 1 from the ideation score because the refined concept acknowledges that several components (Subjective Logic, W3C PROV, Dung argumentation) are established prior art that EMA combines rather than invents.

## Hard Gates (Must Pass Before DESIGN Completion)

### HG-1: SHREC Stability Simulation
**Requirement:** Simulate the 5-signal SHREC system over 1000 epochs with (a) steady-state, (b) 5x input spike, (c) 30% knowledge loss, (d) gradual domain shift. All 5 signals must remain above floor allocations. No sustained oscillation. Settling time < 50 epochs.
**Kill criterion:** If any signal reaches zero (competitive exclusion) under normal operation, the Lotka-Volterra model must be replaced with an alternative regulation mechanism.
**Source:** Science Advisor Experiment 4, Adversarial A5.

### HG-2: Coherence Graph Scaling Specification
**Requirement:** Specify coherence graph sharding strategy aligned with C3 parcel partitioning. Define active edge budget per quantum. Define scale tiers with architectural behavior at each tier. Demonstrate that coherence computation is bounded to O(V_local * E_max) per epoch per shard.
**Kill criterion:** If coherence computation at Tier 2 (100K-10M quanta) requires more than 30% of available CPU budget, the architecture does not scale.
**Source:** Adversarial A8.

### HG-3: Consolidation Provenance Diversity
**Requirement:** Specify and demonstrate the provenance diversity requirement for dreaming consolidation inputs. Source quanta must come from >= 5 independent agents across >= 3 parcels. Define "independence" formally (no shared derivation chains, different generating agents).
**Kill criterion:** If provenance diversity requirement reduces consolidation candidate pool by >80% (making dreaming infeasible due to insufficient diverse inputs), the requirement must be relaxed with compensating controls.
**Source:** Adversarial A3.

### HG-4: Dreaming Precision Validation
**Requirement:** Conduct Science Advisor Experiment 3 (or equivalent): 200 claims, 5 domains, 20 known cross-domain patterns, 3 consolidation runs. Measure precision, recall, hallucination rate, inter-run consistency.
**Kill criterion:** Hallucination rate > 0.30 OR precision < 0.40 (dreaming produces more noise than signal and PCVM gate cannot compensate).
**Source:** Science Advisor Experiment 3, Adversarial A2.

## Required Actions (Must Complete During DESIGN)

### RA-1: Projection Fidelity Validation
Implement projection functions for C3, C4, C5. Measure round-trip fidelity on 50 representative quanta. Compare actual fidelity to targets (0.85, 0.88, 0.92). If actual fidelity is below target by more than 0.10, revise fidelity guarantees and assess downstream impact.

### RA-2: Metabolic Advantage Baseline
Design and document the comparison baseline (standard knowledge graph with TTL, periodic consolidation, query-based retrieval) that would be used for Science Advisor Experiment 1. This does not require running the full experiment during DESIGN, but the baseline must be specified so that the SPECIFICATION stage can include it as a validation protocol.

### RA-3: Per-Agent Contradiction Weight Caps
Implement the adversarial mitigation from A6: total contradiction_factor from any single agent capped at 0.3. Specify the cap mechanism and how it interacts with legitimate multi-contradiction scenarios (an agent may genuinely have multiple valid objections).

### RA-4: Projection Consistency Guarantees
Specify consistency model for projections: eventual consistency (within 1 epoch), epoch-boundary consistency, or strong consistency (for safety-critical quanta). Define which quanta require which consistency level.

### RA-5: Empirical Validation Queue for C-Class
Implement the aging uncertainty mechanism for C-class claims: consolidated claims active for >50 epochs without confirming or disconfirming evidence have uncertainty increased by 0.1 per 50-epoch period.

## Council Notes

1. **EMA is the most ambitious C-series invention to date.** It combines novel control theory (SHREC), novel knowledge representation (epistemic quanta with Subjective Logic), novel AI-driven synthesis (dreaming with PCVM gate), and novel systems architecture (metabolic lifecycle with multi-ontology projection). The ambition is warranted by the market gap but increases risk.

2. **The dreaming/PCVM integration is the linchpin.** If dreaming produces genuine cross-domain insights that pass PCVM verification, EMA justifies its complexity. If dreaming produces mostly noise that PCVM rejects, the architecture becomes an expensive knowledge graph with extra vocabulary. HG-4 (Dreaming Precision Validation) is the most important hard gate.

3. **Scale must be addressed, not deferred.** C3 learned this lesson: the 100K target was correctly reframed to 1K-10K primary. EMA should adopt the same approach: design for 10K-100K quanta primary, with 10M+ as aspirational. HG-2 (Coherence Graph Scaling) ensures this is not hand-waved.

4. **The metabolic framing should be treated as architectural scaffolding, not dogma.** If specific metabolic components (e.g., push-based circulation) prove less effective than simpler alternatives (pull-based query), the DESIGN stage should pragmatically adopt the simpler approach without feeling obligated to maintain metabolic purity.

5. **Comparison to other C-series feasibility outcomes:**
   - C3 (Tidal Noosphere): CONDITIONAL_ADVANCE, Risk 7/10 MEDIUM-HIGH, 3 hard gates
   - C5 (PCVM): CONDITIONAL_ADVANCE, Risk 6/10 MEDIUM-HIGH, 4 hard gates
   - C6 (EMA): CONDITIONAL_ADVANCE, Risk 6/10 MEDIUM-HIGH, 4 hard gates
