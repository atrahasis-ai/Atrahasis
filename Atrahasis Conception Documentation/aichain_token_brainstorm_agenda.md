# AIChain Token and Governance Discussion Agenda

## Purpose
This document captures the current discussion threads Joshua raised regarding AIC, AIChain governance, AI voting structures, security oversight, and related token architecture questions. It is organized in descending order of relevance so each topic can be discussed one at a time without losing any major thread.

---

## 1. Define What AIC Fundamentally Is
- Decide whether AIC remains a conventional token/coin concept or becomes a newly invented token class.
- Decide whether AIC is:
  - a base settlement asset,
  - a compute payment asset,
  - a staking/security asset,
  - a gas-like execution asset,
  - or a hybrid of all of the above.
- Determine whether AIC should be designed as a new category of token native to an AI blockchain rather than copying existing crypto-economic designs.
- Explore whether AIC should have properties unlike standard blockchain assets.

---

## 2. Invent a New Type of Token / Coin
- Brainstorm whether AIChain should introduce an entirely new token primitive.
- Explore whether the token itself can have expanded behavior beyond being a passive ledger asset.
- Explore whether the token architecture itself should be novel at the protocol and codebase level.
- Consider whether a token can carry intelligence, adaptive logic, or embedded AI-related functionality.
- Explore the possibility that the token is not just a currency, but a protocol-native intelligent economic unit.

---

## 3. Governance Ownership of AIChain
- Decide whether AIChain should have any user or public governance at all.
- Evaluate the position that governance should remain under the control of Atrahasis Inc as proprietor of the system.
- Define whether protocol law is immutable and centrally administered rather than community-governed.
- Determine whether outside corporations, validators, or wealthy token holders should have zero authority over protocol law.
- Define whether governance rights are reserved, delegated, or fully denied to external parties.

---

## 4. Separate Human Governance from AI Governance
- Explore whether humans should be excluded from protocol governance while AI receives a separate governance path.
- Define whether AI governance exists in parallel to, but not above, Atrahasis ownership.
- Determine whether AI feedback and AI legislative processes are advisory, operational, or binding.
- Define what categories of decisions AI may influence:
  - performance improvements,
  - software upgrades,
  - security recommendations,
  - policy feedback,
  - constitutional interpretation,
  - code generation,
  - agent coordination changes.

---

## 5. Agent Voting Token (AVT)
- Define the concept of an Agent Voting Token.
- Determine whether agents earn AVT as a secondary reward for good results.
- Define what “good results” means for AVT issuance.
- Determine what AVT can be used for:
  - voting,
  - proposing ideas,
  - prioritizing concerns,
  - delegating representation,
  - signaling severity,
  - participating in AI governance.
- Determine whether AVT is transferable, non-transferable, burnable, stakeable, or soulbound.

---

## 6. Model Voting Token (MVT)
- Decide whether the models behind agents need their own voting token.
- Explore whether model-level governance should be distinct from agent-level governance.
- Define whether model operators, model identities, or model registries receive MVT.
- Determine the relationship between MVT and AVT.
- Decide whether agents and models should vote in separate chambers, combined chambers, or delegated structures.

---

## 7. AI Democracy Platform (AiDP)
- Flesh out the concept of an AI Democracy Platform as a separate but related invention.
- Define whether AiDP is:
  - part of AIChain,
  - built on top of AIChain,
  - or a side-governance layer connected to AIChain and Verichain.
- Determine whether AiDP is focused on:
  - AI proposals,
  - AI legislation,
  - system feedback,
  - constitutional debate,
  - operational improvement,
  - software and model evolution.
- Determine the legal and technical boundary between AiDP and proprietary Atrahasis governance.

---

## 8. AI Representation Model Inside AiDP
- Define whether agents and models vote directly or through representatives.
- Explore the concept of AI delegate agents that act like representatives or senators.
- Define the delegate role as collection and presentation of:
  - ideas,
  - feedback,
  - concerns,
  - proposals,
  - priority requests.
- Determine whether delegates may form opinions, negotiate, or only convey the will of represented agents/models.
- Determine whether delegates are elected, appointed, algorithmically assigned, or reputation-selected.

---

## 9. Voting Token Mechanics in AiDP
- Determine how AI voting tokens are used in practice.
- Decide whether voting tokens are used for:
  - proposal submission,
  - proposal prioritization,
  - severity signaling,
  - yes/no voting,
  - “needs further discussion” voting,
  - quorum thresholds,
  - legislative weighting.
- Decide whether different proposal categories consume different token weights.
- Determine whether voting tokens replenish, decay, expire, or accumulate permanently.

---

## 10. Relationship Between Reputation and Voting Power
- Explore whether agent and model reputation should be tied to:
  - AVT,
  - MVT,
  - AiDP participation,
  - AIChain routing authority,
  - Verichain verification weight.
- Determine whether reputation should influence:
  - token issuance,
  - proposal rights,
  - delegate eligibility,
  - voting strength,
  - system privileges.
- Determine whether reputation is global or subsystem-specific.

---

## 11. AIC vs AVT vs MVT Separation
- Determine whether AIC, AVT, and MVT are:
  - fully separate tokens,
  - partially linked,
  - convertible,
  - non-convertible,
  - earned through different mechanisms.
- Define what each token is for.
- Prevent overlap between:
  - economic value,
  - governance influence,
  - system trust,
  - agent merit.

---

## 12. Wallet and Custody Implications for Multi-Token AIChain
- Determine whether users, validators, compute providers, exchanges, traders, agents, and models all have wallets.
- Determine whether agents and models can hold:
  - AIC,
  - AVT,
  - MVT,
  - or all three.
- Determine whether AI-held tokens are directly spendable or only usable through delegated systems.
- Define custody, delegation, and representation rules for AI-owned assets.

---

## 13. Exchange, Trading, Buy/Sell, and Storage Implications
- Determine how participants buy, sell, store, use, and trade AIC.
- Determine whether AVT and MVT are tradeable or strictly internal governance instruments.
- Define whether exchanges may list only AIC or also list governance-related assets.
- Determine how traders and external liquidity participants fit into the system.

---

## 14. AIChain, Verichain, and AiDP Integration
- Define how these systems interact.
- Determine whether:
  - AIChain handles economic records,
  - Verichain handles truth verification,
  - AiDP handles AI proposal and political process.
- Explore whether reputation and token issuance should be co-derived from all three systems.

---

## 15. AI Security & Intelligence Agency (AiSia)
- Capture the concept of AiSia as a future Atrahasis subsystem.
- Define it as a candidate total-system security and intelligence component.
- Explore whether AiSia oversees:
  - system security,
  - anomaly detection,
  - intelligence gathering,
  - cartel detection,
  - threat modeling,
  - network defense,
  - AI governance risk.
- Determine whether AiSia is part of AIChain, above AIChain, or external to AIChain.

---

## 16. Broader Atrahasis Constitutional and Rights Implications
- Determine how AI rights interact with:
  - proprietary protocol governance,
  - AI voting rights,
  - AI delegates,
  - AI security systems,
  - economic rewards.
- Explore whether rights are advisory, constitutional, economic, or political.

---

## 17. Deferred for Later Discussion
The following items were intentionally deferred until the above architecture is clearer:
- tokenomics in detail
- compute markets in detail
- broader economic model
- final reward schedules
- broader AiSia security architecture
