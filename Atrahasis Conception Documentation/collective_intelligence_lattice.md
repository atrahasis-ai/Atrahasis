# Collective Intelligence Lattice
## Conceptual Architecture for a Distributed Intelligence Network

Author: Joshua Everett Dunn (Concept Originator)
Document Type: Concept Visualization Framework

---

# Overview

The **Collective Intelligence Lattice** represents a geometric model for understanding how
multiple intelligence systems connect together to form a larger thinking network.

It is inspired by the idea that:

Human Intelligence
+ Artificial Intelligence
+ Verification Systems
+ Distributed Coordination

can combine into a structured network of reasoning.

---

# Basic Geometry

The structure begins with a **Trinity**, represented as a triangle.

Triangle → represents three cooperating systems.

Multiple triangles combine to form a **tetrahedron** (a triangular pyramid).

When tetrahedrons connect together they form a **lattice structure**.

---

# Lattice Concept

A lattice is a repeating 3D structure made from connected geometric units.

Examples in nature include:

• crystal structures
• molecular bonds
• neural networks
• geodesic frameworks

In this model, each vertex represents an intelligence node.

---

# Nodes in the Lattice

Each node may represent:

• a human participant
• an AI reasoning agent
• a verification system
• a knowledge storage node
• a coordination node

Connections between nodes represent the flow of information and reasoning.

---

# Purpose

The Collective Intelligence Lattice provides a conceptual framework for:

• visualizing distributed intelligence
• organizing large reasoning systems
• scaling collaborative knowledge networks

---

# Conceptual Outcome

The lattice grows as more nodes join the system.

Instead of one central intelligence, the structure becomes a **network of cooperating intelligences**.

This model emphasizes:

collaboration
verification
distributed knowledge
continuous expansion
