## 4. Adversarial Analyst — Counter-Report

### 4.1 The Case for Abandoning C16

1. **Premature outreach is worse than no outreach.** If Atrahasis approaches Oxford GovAI before having a funded entity, operational team, or demonstrated capability beyond documents, the institution may form a negative first impression that is difficult to reverse.

2. **The 21-year commitment ask is absurd for first contact.** No rational institutional leader will sign a 21-year binding governance commitment with an entity that has no legal existence. The tiered pathway mitigates this but the eventual ask remains extraordinary.

3. **Joshua Dunn has no established academic reputation in AI governance.** Without institutional affiliation, publication record, or endorsement from recognized researchers, the outreach letter may never reach decision-makers.

4. **Alternative: "Publish first" track.** Publish the governance design through peer-review channels. Let the paper establish credibility. Approach institutions only after publication and demonstrated interest from the governance community.

### 4.2 Adversarial Recommendation
**CONDITIONAL — proceed with outreach package but add a parallel "publish first" track.** The governance paper (Component 2) should be simultaneously submitted for academic publication. This creates a credibility backstop: even if direct outreach fails, a published paper establishes the appointment problem as a recognized contribution.
